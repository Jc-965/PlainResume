How to write your resume

        
      

      

        
PlainResume formats plain text as you type. There is no syntax to learn, and messy spacing is fine. These light conventions just give the parser a clearer signal.


        
Header

        
    
          
  • Put your name on the first line.
    • 
          
  • Put contact details on the next line, separated by |, for example email | website | phone | city.
    • 
          
  • A short line right after that becomes your headline or summary.
    • 
        


        
Sections

        
    
          
  • Start a section with its own heading line, such as EXPERIENCE, PROJECTS, EDUCATION, or SKILLS.
    • 
          
  • All caps or Title Case both work, with or without a trailing colon.
    • 
        


        
Jobs and projects

        
    
          
  • First line: Role or Project | Dates
    • 
          
  • Second line: Company | Location
    • 
          
  • Then one achievement per line, each starting with -.
    • 
        


        
Skills

        
    
          
  • Use Label: comma, separated, values, for example Languages: TypeScript, Python, SQL.
    • 
        


        
Dates and locations

        
    
          
  • Write dates any way you like, such as May 2025 - Aug 2025 or 2024 - Present. PlainResume moves them into a clean right aligned column for you.
    • 
        


        
Example

        
Mira Vale
mira.vale@example.com | code.example/mira | (555) 010-2486 | Portland, OR
